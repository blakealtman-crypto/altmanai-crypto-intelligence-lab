# Risk Disclosure Framework

This project organizes digital asset risk into eight categories. This is an educational framework for thinking about risk — not an exhaustive list, and not a substitute for professional financial or legal advice.

## 1. Volatility Risk
Crypto asset prices can move sharply in short periods. Historical volatility does not predict future volatility.

## 2. Liquidity Risk
Some assets are far harder to sell at a fair price than they were to buy — especially during market stress, when liquidity can disappear fastest.

## 3. Custody Risk
Whoever holds the private keys controls the asset. Third-party custodial failures have resulted in total loss of user funds before, and can again.

## 4. Smart Contract Risk
Bugs, exploits, or unaudited logic in smart contracts can cause irreversible loss. An audit reduces but does not eliminate this risk.

## 5. Regulatory Risk
Rules governing digital assets vary by jurisdiction and can change with little notice, affecting access, taxation, and legality of certain activities.

## 6. Emotional Decision Risk
Fear of missing out and panic during drawdowns are common drivers of poor decisions in volatile markets. Plans made in advance tend to hold up better than reactions made under stress.

## 7. Concentration Risk
Large exposure to a single asset, protocol, or platform magnifies the impact of anything going wrong with that one thing. This can be a deliberate, informed choice — the risk is not knowing you've made it.

## 8. Exchange Risk
Centralized exchanges can fail, freeze withdrawals, be hacked, or become insolvent. A balance held on an exchange is a claim on that exchange, not direct ownership of the underlying asset — exchange failures are a well-documented historical risk, not a hypothetical one.
